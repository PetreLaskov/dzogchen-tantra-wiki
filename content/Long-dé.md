# Long-dé

**Long-dé** (Tib. *klong sDe* — "Space series") is the second of the three series of Dzogchen.

## Key Points

- **The three Dzogchen series.** Dzogchen is traditionally divided into three:
  1. **[[Sem-dé]]** (*sems sde*) — the Mind series. The one developed in *Roaring Silence*; the Four Naljors are its ngöndro.
  2. **Long-dé** (*klong sde*) — the Space series. Ch.13 Glossary definition: *"The Series of Space, one of the three series of Dzogchen."*
  3. **[[Me-ngag-dé]]** (*man ngag sde*) — the Series of Implicit Instruction (named for the first time in the Ch.13 Glossary).
- **Ch.2's sole mention.** The *gom-tag* (meditation strap, three cloth bands in blue/red/white) is named as "used in the practice of Dzogchen Long-dé." This is the first time the book distinguishes Long-dé from Sem-dé; no elaboration of content yet.
- **Gom-tag detail.** The Ch.2 gomchenma wears "an embroidered gom-tag across her chest — parallel bands of swirling clouds in blue, red, and white." The strap is a Long-dé practice-support. Its presence in her costume signals her practice spans beyond Sem-dé.
- **Yogic colors.** Blue, red, and white are "the yogic colors" — they correspond across several Dzogchen and Vajrayana practice contexts (ngakpa/ngakma ordination robes, trikaya symbolism, element associations). The gom-tag is one instance.

## Why Ch.2 Bothers to Surface It

The book's structural spine is Sem-dé (via the Four Naljors). But the *Roaring Silence* method does not claim Sem-dé is *all* of Dzogchen; it is one of three doors. The gom-tag reference is a small, deliberate signal: the gomchenma practices Long-dé too, the tradition is larger than Sem-dé alone, and the reader should register that Sem-dé is *a* series, not *the* series.

## What Long-dé Is (Beyond the Book's Scope, Flagged)

In Nyingma Dzogchen literature broadly, Long-dé is associated with the posture-based practices that use physical supports (including the gom-tag meditation strap) to stabilize the practitioner in *longs-sku* (the Space dimension) while working with *tiglé* and specific yogic positions. *Roaring Silence* does not teach Long-dé and this page stops there; the Ch.13 Glossary confirms the series but does not elaborate the content — "The Series of Space" is the full extent of the gloss.

## The Three Series Finalized

With Ch.13 named, all three Dzogchen series are now present in the wiki: [[Sem-dé]] (the book's primary scope), Long-dé, and [[Me-ngag-dé]] (newly added). Long-dé remains a placeholder that prevents the [[Sem-dé]] page from implying Sem-dé = Dzogchen and that anchors the gom-tag reference in Ch.2.
