## [2026-04-22] query | Lion's Roar metaphor — deep reading

User asked what *seng-gé'i dra* ("the lion's roar of reality") is intended to do as a metaphor. The [[Lion's Roar of Reality]] concept page gave the skeleton; the query asked to *dig deep*. Per CLAUDE.md §Query step 4, the reusable answer was filed as an analysis page.

**Created**: [[Lion's Roar - Deep Reading]] (type: analysis, importance 5, confidence probable, status 🌱).

Six moves unpacked:
1. *Why a lion vs. alternatives* — lion's roar is non-instrumental (voice = being); contrast the dog's referential bark. Not in the concept page; important for establishing why this metaphor and not another.
2. *Roaring silence = propositionlessness, not soundlessness* — a proposition needs subject/predicate which need [[Reference Points]]; what is proclaimed has no exterior vantage from which to be predicated.
3. *The chiasmus is load-bearing* — *self-existent* on both sides of "proclamation of confidence" equates medium and content ontologically, closing every exit to instrumentality.
4. *"Taken refuge in timidity" inverts the Buddhist refuge formula* — diagnosis: reference-point dependence as hiding-place. Wordplay surgical, not rhetorical.
5. *"Of reality," not "of the Dharma"* — sharpens Dzogchen's stance beyond the inherited Mahāyāna *siṃhanāda* (Śrīmālādevī, Mahāparinirvāṇa, Siṃhanāda sūtras); the genitive collapses.
6. *Practical entailment — subtractive path* — because the roar is continuously sounding, no generating-practice can produce it; [[Shi-nè]] and the [[Four Naljors]] exist to loosen timidity, not to build confidence from scratch.

**Touched**: [[Lion's Roar of Reality]] (added Related link to deep-reading page). Index: new Recent Activity entry, new entry under Domain: Dzogchen.

Confidence set to *probable* because the analysis extends beyond textual claims into comparative (Mahāyāna siṃhanāda context) and structural (chiasmus, inverted-refuge) inference — well-supported but interpretive.
